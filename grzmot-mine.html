Grzmot Mine Prop

This project was a commission by a friend for her 'Ela' cosplay from the game Rainbow Six: Siege. The 'Grzmot Mine' is this operative's signature utility.

The project consisted of three main phases. Design, printing, and post processing.

Three mines in their holsters.

Design Phase

The design of the mine required six separate models to be made. The handle, battery compartment, pin guard, pin, holster, and belt clip.

The handle is the main component, which was modelled in detail from several reference images taken in-game. The battery compartment holds a single 9 volt battery and screws into the bottom of the handle.

3D model of the handle and holster.

The void in the holster would be filled by a foam filter fitted around the battery compartment.

Accounting for the dimensions of the pin hole in the handle, the pin and pin guard were then designed separately from the rest of the project. The belp clip was an existing design modified to fit the circumference of the holster.

Printing Phase

Printing was the single longest phase of the project, with the holster and handle taking nearly 12 hours each at maximum precision. Between all parts, each mine would require around 30 hours of print time, meaning 90 hours for the entire set.

After testing the threading of the handle and cap and ensuring the battery would fit snugly with a prototype, small design changes were made and the final version of all piece were ready to print.

Handle and battery compartment prototype with test display casting.

Post Processing

Several parts needed processing after production. The filter and pin guard were sprayed arctic gray, the pin silver. Then the pin and handle touched up with orange acrylic paint.

The illuminated displays were then cast out of hot glue and inserted in the side and top of the handle. finally the electronics were mounted inside and the first mine was done.
