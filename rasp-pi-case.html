Raspberry Pi Case

This project was a personal project of mine that involved modifying a model of a case for a Raspberry Pi computer that would help the device fill its role as a voice assistant I programmed.

The case needed to meet several criteria. It needed to allow internal access to the USB ports and have enough room the microphone to fit comfortably. The case also needed holes for an indicator light and the microphone, as well as external access to the ethernet port, which was on the same face as the USB.

The final product.

Printing

Initially, I was going to print the case in black acrylonitrile butadiene styrene, since ABS is more resistant to heat than typical filament. However I came across woodfill filament and decided to go crazy with it.

After testing the threading of the handle and cap and ensuring the battery would fit snugly with a prototype, small design changes were made and the final version of all piece were ready to print.

First layer going down.

Finished print.

It looks vaguely wood-ish? Definitely smells like it.

Finishing

Lots of stringing here but that's to be expected with all the wood fiber in there. Next step is to sand along the "grain" and give it a few coats of stain and see if it takes it like real wood.

Sanded and 2 coats of polystain.

It does. It really brings out the color in the burnt sawdust/plastic hybrid. Applied correctly the stain even gives the wood an illusion of grain that wasn't even there in the first place.

The hole for the LED was then given a cast hot glue diffuser and it was ready for the electronics (after 2-4 weeks because the off-gassing stain was nauseating). Note the difference between the outside and inside, that's just two coats!

Raspberry Pi 3B+ in along with the microphone from an old webcam.
